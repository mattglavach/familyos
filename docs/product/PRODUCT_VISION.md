# Product Vision

## Mission
Family OS helps one household manage daily life, shared responsibilities, home operations, planning, and long-term decisions from one trusted place.

## Vision
Family OS becomes the single trusted operating system for managing every aspect of our family's life and our home.

## North Star
Family OS is the single trusted operating system for managing every aspect of our family's life and our home.

Every product decision should support this statement. Features that do not make household life clearer, safer, faster, or more coordinated should be deferred.

## Target Audience
- The primary household owner and power user.
- A spouse or adult collaborator who needs simple, reliable access.
- Teenagers and children with age-appropriate access to tasks, schedules, and family context.
- Future guests or external collaborators with limited, clearly separated access.

## Primary Goals
- Reduce missed responsibilities.
- Reduce mental load.
- Make household work visible and shared.
- Centralize family information that is currently scattered across memory, apps, messages, notes, calendars, and spreadsheets.
- Make daily planning fast enough to use every morning.
- Preserve household privacy and trust.

## Non-Goals
- Do not become a public social network.
- Do not optimize for generic enterprise project management.
- Do not require heavy configuration before value appears.
- Do not expose private household data to guests, integrations, or AI features without explicit design and approval.
- Do not add broad product domains before the core household operating loop is stable.

## Success Metrics
- The app is opened most days for a quick household check-in.
- The dashboard answers "what needs attention today?" without extra searching.
- Tasks are created, assigned, completed, and trusted by more than one household member.
- Calendar, household, and settings states are understandable without developer context.
- Mobile use feels fast, readable, and low-friction.
- Family members report fewer missed tasks, reminders, and handoffs.

See `docs/product/SUCCESS_METRICS.md` for the durable metrics list.

## Core Product Principles
- Household-first: prioritize real household workflows over abstract platform features.
- Trust-first: privacy, access control, and data correctness are product requirements.
- Action-oriented: every screen should make the next useful action obvious.
- Low cognitive load: use simple language, familiar patterns, and progressive disclosure.
- Shared by default where appropriate: household information should be visible to the right people.
- Personal where needed: defaults, assignments, and filters should respect individual context.
- Mobile-first: the app should work well during real life, not only at a desk.
- Incremental: add modules when the foundation can support them.

## Long-Term Vision
Family OS should grow into a durable household command center:
- A morning briefing for daily priorities.
- A shared task and chore system.
- A reliable family calendar surface.
- Household membership, permissions, and settings.
- Planning modules for money, school, travel, documents, home, and life lists.
- Smart home and automation integrations after trust and data boundaries are mature.
- AI assistance that summarizes, suggests, and automates only after human-operated workflows are stable.

## Differentiation
Family OS is not only a family calendar, to-do app, or home inventory. It combines household context, shared operating workflows, long-term planning, private data, and future automation in one controlled system.

Existing family organizer apps tend to focus on calendars, chores, shopping, or messaging. Family OS is designed as a modular household operating system where each module shares the same household identity, permissions, design language, and daily dashboard.

## Release 1.0 Alignment
Release 1.0 should focus only on the core operating loop:
- Dashboard.
- Tasks.
- Calendar status.
- Household management.
- Settings.
- Navigation.
- Notifications/status feedback.
- Responsive UI and UX polish.

Shopping, AI, Smart Home, Meal Planning, Inventory, and Home integrations remain deferred because they depend on a stable core experience, clear module boundaries, and trusted household data. Life Lists becomes active in Release 1.1 as a generic lightweight planning module.
